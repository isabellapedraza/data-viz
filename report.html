Subtheme: Housing for the Rich

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. What are the profiles of buyers and sellers in the real estate market, including the types of homes they typically buy or sell?
      2. Motivation: After reading Homes for a Profit, I've realized that a large amount of housing is being bought up by investors. If homes are being sold for a profit like any other asset – such as a shares in a company – is the profit being shared across individuals and investors, or are investors the primary generators and benefactors of profit? Is the market slowly being dominated to only pass hands between investors, in the same way stocks are usually held, bought, and sold by large financial institutions?
  2. For the years 2019-2022, how many residential and commercial property sales in Boston would have qualified for a transfer fee under Healey's Affordable Homes Act?
      3. Motivation: As we saw in the article Reckoning with Boston's Towers of Wealth, Mayor Wu's proposed transfer fee on residential and commercial property sales over $2 million would have generated $384 million in potential transfer fee revenue across the years 2019-2022. Last year, Governor Healey proposed a more "aggressive" bill that would create a tiered transfer fee on real-estate sales above $1 million or the county's median home price, whichever is higher, to support affordable housing. The fee would only apply to the part of the sale that exceeds those levels. Is this bill actually more aggressive than Mayor Wu's? How much more revenue would actually be generated under Healey's bill?
  3. How does the profit margin compare between selling luxury homes versus non-luxury homes?
      4. Motivation: As we saw in the article Reckoning with Boston's Towers of Wealth, developers in Boston have mostly focused on creating luxury housing over affordable housing. I'm curious – are they really making that much more profit from luxury homes when buildings, such as One Dalton, still have unsold units? Wouldn't it make more sense to create profit from volume by buying and selling moderately priced homes?
